MVP Statistics by Year: PPG, RPG, APG

The chart above displays three different line graphs: points per game, rebounds per game, and assists per game. The legend in the upper-right corner explains this.

Points per game and assists per game have remained relatively stable in terms of trend lines throughout the history of the NBA. There are fluctuations year-over-year, here does not appear to be a significant trend in either direction. However, rebounds per game by the eventual MVP winner have significantly decreased on a year-over-year basis.

The decline in rebounds per game could be attributed to many factors. A few such factors include, faster style of gameplay with less emphasis on rebounding, a focus on playing "small-ball" and from the perimeter, greater competition, and more NBA teams to name a few. No one factor can be attributed to the decline in rebounds per game by the MVP. Additional analysis would need to be performed to make a conclusion.
