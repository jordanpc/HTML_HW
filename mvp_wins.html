MVP Wins by Positional Group

There are three buckets of player positions in the NBA: 1) Guards, 2) Forwards, and 3) Centers. The guards can be further broken down into Point Guards and Shooting Guards. Likewise, forwards have sub-groups of Small Forwards and Power Forwards. Some players also have the designation of Forward-Center.

As the data highlights, just over one-third (36.1%) of all MVP winners have played the Center position during their winning campaign. Greater than one-half (57.4%) of players to have won the MVP award fall into the category of "big men", or players who were either Centers (C), Forward-Centers (FC), or Power Forwards (PF).
